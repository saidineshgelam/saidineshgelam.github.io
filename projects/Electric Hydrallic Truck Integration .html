Integration of Electric Hydraulic Truck (65-Ton Dump Truck)

Electric Vehicle Conversion | SolidWorks | ANSYS Structural, Modal, Harmonic | Cooling, Controls, and Mechanical Integration

Project Overview

This project involved converting a 65-ton diesel hydraulic dump truck into a fully electric heavy-duty vehicle. The effort spanned across mechanical, electrical, hydraulic, and thermal domains, with a strong focus on structural integrity, cooling performance, and system-level reliability.

I led the integration effort, covering everything from mounting bracket design and thermal circuit layout to motor selection, vibration analysis, and real-world testing. The final EV system delivered high-efficiency propulsion and precise hydraulic operation, operating under rough terrain and shock conditions up to 10g.

Mechanical Design & Structural Integration

Mounting Brackets & GD&T
  • Designed structural mounting brackets for propulsion motor, gearbox, inverters, pumps, and auxiliaries using SolidWorks
  • Used GD&T methodology to control critical alignment between motor and mechanical drivetrain shafts
  • Integrated damping materials and stress-relief profiles into brackets for durability
Structural & Vibration Simulation
  • Performed FEA (Static, Modal, Harmonic) analysis in ANSYS to simulate loading under shock and terrain impact (10g)
  • Mapped natural frequencies using modal analysis to avoid coupling with road-induced vibration
  • Validated structural margins of safety and fatigue life using iterative redesign cycles

Subsystems: Propulsion & Hydraulic Actuators

Propulsion Motor Selection & Integration
  • Selected a high-torque electric motor for heavy-duty applications with low-speed, high-load characteristics
  • Designed mechanical interfaces to integrate motor with the drivetrain and aligned it with inverter control strategies
Auxiliary Motors & Hydraulic Actuators
  • Integrated electric hydraulic pump motors to power lifting mechanisms
  • Included electric steering motor and coolant pump motors into high-voltage bus
  • Ensured thermal isolation and efficient control of each actuator under combined duty cycles

Testing & Validation

  • Bench-tested all subsystem mounts using prototype assemblies with simulated 10g impact profiles
  • Validated complete coolant flow with embedded sensors logging flow rate, pressure drop, and return temperatures
  • Conducted DFMEA sessions on mechanical, hydraulic, and thermal interfaces to address early-stage risk mitigation
  • Performed field trials with data logging: structural vibration, motor torque response, coolant loop behavior

Tools & Technologies

  • SolidWorks for 3D modeling, assembly layout, and bracket design
  • ANSYS (Structural, Modal, Harmonic) for mechanical simulation and vibration mapping
  • DFMEA and FEA for risk mitigation and structural load validation
  • Custom test rigs used for bracket strength, vibration testing, and coolant circuit tuning

Project Outcome

  • Successfully converted a 65-ton hydraulic diesel truck into a full-electric vehicle
  • Maintained mechanical safety margins under off-road conditions
  • Enabled thermal-safe operation of motors, inverters, and pumps via coolant circuit optimization
  • Achieved field-readiness with successful real-world trials and logging
