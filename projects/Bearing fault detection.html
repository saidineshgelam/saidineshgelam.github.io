Fault Diagnosis of Rotor-Bearing System Using Machine Learning

Vibration Signal Processing | PCA | SOM | Logistic Regression | Fault Classification

Project Overview

A rotor-bearing testbed was constructed to evaluate the health condition of a rotating shaft system under different unbalance scenarios. The system was tested at a rotational speed of 20 Hz, with additional screws mounted on the disk to induce controlled unbalance defects. Vibration signals were collected using an accelerometer placed on the bearing block. The data acquisition rate was 2560 Hz, under both balanced and unbalanced operating conditions.

Data Acquisition & Experimental Setup

  • Rotor operated at a fixed speed of 20 Hz
  • Screws used to introduce unbalanced conditions at varying levels
  • Accelerometer mounted on bearing block to capture vibrations
  • Signal collected at 2560 Hz under healthy and faulty (unbalanced) conditions

Feature Extraction & Signal Analysis

  • Principal Component Analysis (PCA) applied to identify significant vibration features
  • 1X and 2X harmonic components identified as the most relevant indicators of rotor imbalance
  • RMS and standard deviation were found to contribute minimally to classification

Machine Learning Models

Self-Organizing Maps (SOM)
  • SOM model trained to classify rotor health into three categories: healthy, unbalance 1, and unbalance 2
  • Successfully clustered test samples based on harmonic feature inputs
  • Prediction confidence values ranged from 1.0 (healthy) to 0.6 (unbalance 2), showing lower reliability under severe faults
Logistic Regression
  • Trained on the same feature set as the SOM model
  • Effectively predicted labels for all classes with distinct output values:
    • ~0.75 for healthy
    • ~0.375 for unbalance 1
    • ~0.0 for unbalance 2
  • Offered clearer boundary separation between fault conditions

Conclusion

  • Both SOM and Logistic Regression models provided effective classification of rotor health conditions
  • PCA helped simplify the feature space, highlighting the importance of harmonic frequencies over time-domain metrics
  • The study demonstrated the potential of machine learning in early detection of unbalance faults in rotating machinery
