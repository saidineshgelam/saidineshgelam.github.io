5MW PMSM Motor Design Project

Thermal, Structural, and Electromagnetic Co-Design Using ANSYS, SolidWorks, MotorCAD, and MATLAB

Project Overview

The goal was to design a high-performance 5MW Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor (PMSM) to replace an existing 3.5MW motor with strict constraints: same physical footprint, use of the existing external cooling infrastructure, and a thermal hotspot limit of 140¬∞C on windings in steady-state. Reliability was prioritized by reducing coolant joints and designing the motor to withstand mechanical shocks during transport and operation.

System-Level Design Approach

The entire motor system was modeled and validated through iterative cycles of CAD, FEA, thermal CFD, and hand calculations. Focus areas included electromagnetic losses, component-level heat transfer, vibration response, and structural integrity under extreme loading conditions.

üß© Component Breakdown

  • Stator: Built with 35PN270 lamination steel and housed water jackets for active cooling.
  • Rotor: Integrated permanent magnets and fan blades at ends for induced air cooling; shaft supported by hydrodynamic bearings.
  • Shaft & End Plates: Hand-calculated for deflection, stress, and fatigue using MATLAB and validated through FEA.
  • Cold Plate: Custom machined aluminum cold plate with high-efficiency water flow channels; 25 LPM coolant flow.
  • Insulation System: Multi-layer slot liners (NKN), porabond, polyamide tapes, and thermal interface gel to minimize thermal resistance.

A complete parametric model was created in SolidWorks including all major assemblies and detailed parts. Constraints were maintained for foot-mounting, shaft alignment, and integration with the unchangeable external cooler.

üßä Thermal Management ‚Äì ANSYS Fluent & Thermal

  • CFD was used to simulate internal air motion and rotor-induced air churning, improving convective heat transfer at the end windings.
  • Water jacket geometry optimized for turbulent flow and even temperature distribution.
  • 30¬∞ stator segment used for simulation (cyclic symmetry); real material properties modeled including copper anisotropy.
  • Critical boundary conditions: 40¬∞C inlet temperature, 25 LPM cold plate, 150 LPM external flow through heat exchanger.

üõ†Ô∏è Structural & Modal FEA ‚Äì ANSYS Mechanical

  • Static Structural: Validated stress/strain in shaft, mounts, and frame. Peak stresses remained under yield limits for all loading cases.
  • Shock Load Simulation: 30g acceleration applied in multiple directions. Design confirmed safe for handling and transportation.
  • Modal Analysis: Ensured natural frequencies were far from operational excitation frequencies to prevent resonance.
  • Harmonic Response: Verified displacement and stress under vibrational loads.

Results

  • Max winding temperature: 140¬∞C (within safe limits)
  • Avg winding temp: 130¬∞C
  • Core temp: Avg 92¬∞C
  • Housing temp: 51¬∞C
  • Internal air temp: 76.6¬∞C
  • Magnet temp: Max 132.3¬∞C
  • Shock safety: Passed 30g load simulation
  • Structural margins: Yield factor of safety > 1.8

Key Achievements

  • Increased power by 43% while maintaining original motor footprint
  • Maintained thermal stability using optimized cold plate and internal rotor airflow
  • Reduced coolant joint complexity, enhancing reliability
  • Validated full structural integrity across operational and shock environments
