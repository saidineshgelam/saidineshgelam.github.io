Cold Plates, Heat Sinks, and Ducts for Cooling Inverters

Thermal & Structural Engineering | ANSYS Fluent, Mechanical | CATIA V5 | CFD + FEA + GD&T

Project Overview

This project involved the design, simulation, and validation of thermal management components for high-power inverter systems. The focus was on creating robust, efficient cooling solutions—including cold plates, heat sinks, and duct systems—for IGBT-based motor control units. Designs were optimized for high-volume manufacturing processes while meeting strict reliability and thermal performance targets.

Thermal Management Engineering

  • Developed liquid-cooled cold plates and extruded aluminum air-cooled heat sinks for IGBT modules under high thermal load
  • Focused on minimizing thermal resistance, improving surface contact, and optimizing geometry within packaging constraints
  • Designed airflow ducting systems for air-cooled inverters to enhance flow directionality and reduce recirculation losses

Simulation-Driven Design

  • Used ANSYS Fluent to perform CFD simulations on cold plates and ducted heat sinks
  • Simulated heat dissipation, hotspot mapping, flow uniformity, and pressure drop across complex geometries
  • Conducted FEA (Thermal and Structural) simulations to analyze thermal expansion, clamping stress, and structural deformation during operation
  • Evaluated material performance under thermal cycling for fatigue and long-term reliability

Design for Manufacturability

  • Designed cold plates for compatibility with extrusion, die casting, and injection molding processes
  • Considered tolerance stack-up, wall thickness limits, and cooling channel manufacturability in early CAD stages
  • Optimized fin profiles, microchannels, and mounting features for tool-friendly production

Testing & Collaboration

  • Worked with hardware, thermal, and manufacturing teams to build and test full prototype assemblies
  • Validated thermal performance metrics against simulation data and refined geometry iteratively
  • Focused on minimizing junction temperature, reducing thermal resistance, and improving uniformity across mounting zones

Design Tools & Techniques

  • Used CATIA V5 for all 3D modeling: fin arrays, baseplate pockets, duct contours, and microchannel networks
  • Applied GD&T to control tolerances for mounting bosses, bolt hole patterns, and interface flatness
  • Integrated design features like O-ring grooves, threaded bosses, and quick-connect couplings for leak-proof integration

Outcome

  • Delivered a validated cooling solution used in automotive and industrial inverter platforms
  • Ensured structural integrity, minimal hotspot development, and high system-level thermal reliability
  • Balanced performance, manufacturability, and cost for scalable production
