Research – Core Collapse in Cylindrical Lithium-Ion Batteries

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) | Electrode Mechanics | Lithium-Ion Safety | CALCE, University of Maryland

Project Summary

This research explores the mechanisms behind structural failure—specifically core collapse—within 18650 cylindrical lithium-ion batteries. The collapse is driven by mechanical deformations in the jelly roll structure due to irreversible volume expansion, thermal gradients, and hoop buckling. Conducted at the Center for Advanced Life Cycle Engineering (CALCE), this project integrates material science, mechanical modeling, and battery reliability engineering.

The study combines experimental literature review with analytical modeling and finite element simulations. The key outcome shows that hoop buckling of electrode layers near the battery core—aggravated by cycle-dependent stress accumulation—is a primary cause of jelly roll deformation. This structural failure reduces battery performance and poses risks for internal short circuits.

Key Contributions

  • Conducted detailed literature survey on CT-based structural analysis of Li-ion jelly rolls under cycling
  • Developed Euler and hoop buckling stress models for electrode layers at various radial positions
  • Quantified stress amplification near the core due to compressive radial and hoop loading
  • Identified cycle-induced irreversible volume expansion as a dominant factor in core collapse
  • Suggested mitigation strategies including center pin design, tab placement, and material modifications

Modeling & Analysis

  • Modeled electrodes as cylindrical beams using classical Euler buckling theory and verified with hoop buckling thresholds
  • Stress thresholds near the core found to be 10–50× higher than those simulated under single-cycle models
  • Published CT scan comparisons from literature used to validate failure locations and buckling trends
  • Suggested slippage between jelly roll layers as an unaddressed but critical influence on stress propagation

Tools & Techniques

  • Mathematical modeling of axisymmetric structures using Ansys
  • Finite Element concepts applied to understand stress concentration zones in winding geometry
  • Comparative validation using published high-resolution CT scans and imaging data

Publication & Collaboration

  • Journal: Journal of Power Sources, Elsevier (2024)
  • Authors: Sai Dinesh Gelam, Sahithi Maddipatla, Carmen Chicone, Michael Pecht
  • DOI: 10.1016/j.jpowsour.2024.235471
  • Institution: CALCE – University of Maryland

Impact

This work improves understanding of safety-critical failure modes in cylindrical batteries. It provides guidance for future modeling efforts and manufacturing practices to prevent jelly roll deformation and extend battery lifespan. The findings are applicable to EV battery packs, energy storage systems, and next-generation high-density cell architectures.
