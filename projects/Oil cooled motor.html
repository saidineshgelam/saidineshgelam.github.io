Oil-Cooled Radial Flux Motor – Thermal Reengineering & Power Upgrade

ANSYS Fluent | SpaceClaim | CFD | Thermal Optimization | Direct Oil Cooling

Project Overview

This project focused on upgrading an existing 260 kW radial flux motor by replacing its water-jacket cooling with a direct oil-cooled system. The thermal reengineering enabled the motor to operate at significantly higher current densities and output power while maintaining structural integrity and compact packaging.

A transformer-grade dielectric oil was used as the coolant to directly extract heat from high-loss regions such as stator end windings eliminating the need for end winding potting. The upgraded design successfully dissipated 8 kW of heat while maintaining a maximum hotspot temperature of 110 °C at a flow rate of 60 LPM. This allowed the motor to safely handle continuous current densities as high as 8A/mm² upgrading the power to 380 kW with the same size.

Cooling Architecture & System Conversion

  • Replaced water-jacket cooling with direct oil-injection into stator slot walls and rotor interface zones
  • Directed oil flow toward localized heat sources using custom nozzle placements and internal channeling
  • Improved thermal conduction and minimized resistance across fluid-solid contact surfaces
  • Achieved a compact and scalable thermal design that preserved the motor's outer dimensions

Simulation-Driven Design

  • Prepared detailed fluid-solid CAD model in ANSYS SpaceClaim for thermal-fluid interaction simulation
  • Performed CFD analysis in ANSYS Fluent to evaluate :
    • Oil velocity profiles and turbulence zones
    • Pressure drop across internal channels and nozzles
    • Temperature distribution in copper, steel, and oil
    • Hotspot formation and critical thermal gradient paths
  • Tested multiple cooling topologies :
    • Axial and radial oil jet configurations
    • Embedded cooling channels in stator slots
    • Multi-port oil discharge paths along rotor end faces

Thermal Optimization & Redesign

  • Redesigned oil paths for improved flow uniformity and reduced local overheating
  • Minimized maximum winding temperatures to 110 °C at peak operation
  • Balanced flow rate (60 LPM) against available pump capacity and oil return line pressure
  • Ensured the redesigned system supported continuous current densities of 8 A/mm² without requiring external active cooling
  • Retained rotor-stator mechanical interfaces for seamless integration into the original motor casing

Tools & Techniques

  • ANSYS Fluent for CFD-based thermal-flow analysis
  • ANSYS SpaceClaim for geometry preparation and volume isolation
  • Comparison of multi-topology cooling schemes to guide final design selection

Outcome

  • Delivered a thermally stable, oil-cooled motor design with doubled power capacity
  • Safely operated at high current densities and thermal load without exceeding mechanical or thermal limits
  • Demonstrated how thermal reengineering can unlock next-generation motor performance within legacy form factors
